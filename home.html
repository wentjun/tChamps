"Voluntourism"

￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼￼Most overseas Community Involvement Projects involve “voluntourism”. Under the banner of “making a difference”, students are solicited to travel to developing countries to aid poor communities, such as those in Cambodia, Indonesia, etc. However, in fact, they are more like tourists, enjoying the sights and tastes of the distant and exotic “other” whilst viewing the “experience” pragmatically, as a useful addition to their curriculum vitae.

"Voluntourism"

Research and studies have proven that such projects bring little long-term benedit to their target audience in terms of broadening social networks and capital, or increasing information accessibility. Bridges between the two cultures and communities are rarely formed. Needless to say, emotional care and support are also eschewed in favour of short-term solutions.

Vision

tChamps envisions a sustainable ecosystem for youth empowerment in Asia through ethical overseas community development.

Mission

tChamps supports our overseas educational partner, PSE Cambodia to train their students (aged 18 and above) in community development and youth leadership and help develop and set up a sustainable youth volunteering organisation and system within PSE.

tChamps members will develop leadership and personal skills to become trainers and mentoring partners of Cambodian students for community development projects in Cambodia, so that they may start sustainable community intervention projects within their own communities.

Crucially, tChamps will also advocate thought leadership in the areas of sustainable and ethical overseas community development in a bid against voluntourism. This will be achieved through research, awareness campaigns, forums and thought leadership speakers' series.
